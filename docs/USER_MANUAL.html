🏠What is AlphaQuark?

AlphaQuark is a mobile application built for clients consuming their money manager's service. It bridges your manager's trade recommendations with your stock broker — so you can receive and execute recommendations in real time, subscribe to curated model portfolios, and keep your holdings in sync through periodic rebalancing.

🔌
Connect Your Broker
Securely link one of 12 supported brokers. One tap to re-authenticate when sessions expire.
📊
Stock Recommendations
Receive buy/sell recommendations from your manager and execute them instantly via your connected broker.
🗂️
Model Portfolios
Subscribe to curated strategy baskets. Pay once, execute the initial basket, and keep up with every rebalance signal.
📈
Portfolio P&L
Track live portfolio value, unrealized gains, and historical performance with real-time WebSocket prices.

🔌Connecting a Broker

Before you can execute any trade, you must connect at least one broker. The app supports two connection methods depending on your broker.

1Select Broker
2Enter Credentials
3Authorize via OAuth
4Connected ✓

OAuth Brokers (WebView Login)

For OAuth brokers — Zerodha, Upstox, Fyers, ICICI, Groww, Axis, Motilal — the app opens the broker's own login page inside a secure in-app browser. The app never sees your broker password.

1
Open Broker Connection
Go to Account → Manage Brokers and tap the broker tile. A grid of all 14 supported brokers appears.
2
Enter API Key & Secret
Some OAuth brokers need your developer API key from the broker's portal (Kite developer console for Zerodha, etc). These are encrypted with AES-256 before storage. Groww and Axis skip this — they go directly to step 3.
🌐 Static IP whitelist required for Upstox, Fyers, Groww, ICICI, HDFC, Kotak. Your unique AlphaQuark egress IP appears on this screen — copy it and add to your broker's developer portal whitelist before proceeding.
3
Browser Opens — Log In
A secure in-app WebView opens the broker's login page (Kite / Upstox / Fyers / etc). Enter your broker username, password, and 2FA OTP on the broker's own page.
4
App Intercepts Callback
After login, the broker redirects back to AlphaQuark. The app extracts the auth code from the redirect URL and exchanges it for a session token in the background.
5
Broker Connected ✓
Status updates to Connected. Your session is stored securely and used for all order placement until expiry.

Static IP Whitelisting Requirement

Some brokers require you to whitelist a static IP address in their developer portal before AlphaQuark can place orders on your behalf. This is a one-time setup. Without it, your broker will silently block every order or connection attempt with a "connection timeout" error.

How to Whitelist Your AlphaQuark IP

1
Find Your static IP assigned in the App
When you tap Connect Broker for any whitelist-required broker, the API Key form shows a yellow banner with your unique IP address (e.g. 103.21.142.187) and a Copy button.
2
Open Your Broker's Developer Portal
From a desktop browser, log into your broker's developer/API portal (see table above for URL). Navigate to My Apps or API Keys section.
3
Paste & Save the IP
Find the "Allowed IPs" / "IP Whitelist" / "IP Restrictions" field and paste the IP. Save changes. Most brokers activate the whitelist instantly; a few take 5-10 minutes.
4
Return to AlphaQuark & Connect
Come back to the app, complete the API Key + Secret fields, and proceed with login. The IP whitelist enables every future order placement.
⚠️

Your IP is unique to your AlphaQuark account. Each user gets a dedicated egress IP — never share it or use someone else's. If you delete and re-create your AlphaQuark account, the IP may change and you'll need to re-whitelist.

🚨

Skipping this step causes silent failures. If your IP isn't whitelisted, broker connect or order placement will time out with errors like Couldn't connect to Upstox or upstream_unreachable — even though your credentials are correct. The broker's firewall drops the request without telling AlphaQuark why.

🔄Switching Brokers Mid-Strategy

A common real-world scenario: you started executing a Model Portfolio on Zerodha, but now want to switch to Upstox (or any other broker). Here's exactly what happens.

What Happens When You Switch

1
Connect the New Broker
Go to Account → Manage Brokers → connect Upstox (or your new broker). Your old broker stays connected; both can coexist.
2
Holdings Migration Detected
When you open a Model Portfolio strategy, the app detects the broker switch. A Holdings Migration modal opens automatically.
3
Review Holdings Transfer
The modal lists your existing MP holdings on the old broker. You'll need to physically transfer these stocks to the new broker's demat account, or rebuild the position from cash in the new broker.
4
Confirm Migration
Tap Continue with Upstox. The app creates a new execution record for the new broker. Your old broker's history is preserved but goes inactive for this strategy.
5
Rebalances Now Use New Broker
All future rebalance calculations and order placement happen on the new broker. The new execution record tracks fresh trades, average prices, and P&L.
Click each step to see the screen

What's Preserved vs. What Resets

ItemStatus
Subscription & payment history✅ Preserved (tied to your account, not broker)
Strategy access & rebalance signals✅ Preserved (manager sends to your account)
Old broker's executed trade history✅ Preserved for P&L & tax records
Old broker's holdings on AlphaQuark⚠️ Shown as "previous broker" — read-only
New broker's execution record🆕 Fresh start — initial holdings = whatever you have today in the new broker
Subscription amount basis✅ Carries over for rebalance calculations

Two Ways to Move Holdings

📤
Off-Market Transfer
Use CDSL EASI or your old broker's DIS to transfer stocks to the new broker's demat. Takes 1-2 business days. No tax event. Preserves your average buy price.
💰
Sell + Rebuy
Sell at old broker, withdraw cash, deposit at new broker, rebuy. Faster but creates a taxable event (LTCG/STCG) and triggers brokerage twice.
⚠️

Don't switch mid-rebalance. If a rebalance is pending or partially executed, complete it (or repair it) on the old broker first. Switching mid-flight leaves the rebalance card stuck and requires support intervention.

Disconnecting the Old Broker (Optional)

After migration, you can leave the old broker connected (recommended — preserves access to history) or disconnect it. Disconnecting doesn't delete history, but you lose ability to view live holdings on that broker through AlphaQuark.

💡

Bespoke recommendations are different. For non-MP stock recommendations, the "active broker" is whichever you select at execution time. No migration flow — just pick the broker each time.

📊Stock Recommendations (Bespoke Trades)

Stock Recommendations are individual buy or sell recommendations published by your manager. Each card shows the stock, exchange, action, quantity, and target price.

Executing an Recommendation

1
View the Recommendation Card
On the Home screen, recommendation cards show under "Pending Recommendations". Each card displays the stock, exchange, action (BUY/SELL), suggested quantity, and target price.
2
Open Review Trade Modal
Tap the recommendation. The Review Trade modal shows: stock details, quantity, exchange, estimated cost, available broker funds, and order type (Market / Limit / AMO). Adjust quantity if needed.
3
Sell Authorization Check (SELL only)
For SELL orders, the app verifies DDPI / TPIN / EDIS authorization on your broker. If not set up, a one-time authorization modal appears. Once authorized, you skip this on future sells.
4
Place Order
Tap Place Order. The app validates your broker session, then submits the order. Spinner shows "Placing…" while the broker confirms (5-15 seconds).
5
See the Result
Result displayed: Complete, Pending, or Rejected — with the broker's reason if rejected. Successful orders show actual fill price and quantity.
Click each step to see the screen

📦Order Types Explained

Whenever you execute an recommendation or rebalance, the order is sent with a specific type and variant. Understanding these helps you predict what will happen.

Order Types

TypeMeaningWhen Used
MARKET Buy/sell at the best available price right now Default for most recommendation executions. Fastest fill, no price control.
LIMIT Execute only if price reaches a specified threshold When manager sets a target price; order may sit pending if price doesn't hit.

Order Variants

VariantMeaningWhen Used
REGULAR Standard order during market hours (9:15 AM – 3:30 PM IST) Default when market is open.
AMO After Market Order — queued for the next market open Auto-applied when you place orders outside market hours. Look for the amber AMO badge.

Product Types (Demat vs Intraday)

ProductMeaning
CNC / DELIVERY Stocks delivered to your demat account, held overnight. Used by AlphaQuark for all manager-driven trades.
MIS / INTRADAY Must be squared off before market close. Not used in management flows.
📅

Market hours: 9:15 AM – 3:30 PM IST, Monday to Friday, excluding NSE/BSE holidays. Pre-open session: 9:00 – 9:15 AM (orders accepted, no execution). After-hours: AMO orders accepted 3:45 PM – 8:45 AM next day.

🗂️Model Portfolios

A Model Portfolio (MP) is a strategy curated by your manager — a target basket of stocks with specific weightings. Subscribe to a strategy, invest, and periodically rebalance your holdings to stay aligned with the manager's latest target allocation.

How Model Portfolios Work

Think of a Model Portfolio like a recipe. Your manager maintains the "recipe" (target weights). You invest money to buy those ingredients. When the manager updates the recipe (rebalance event), you buy or sell stocks to match — keeping your actual portfolio mirroring the manager's model.

Three Layers of Data

LayerOwnerWhat It Contains
Model Tier Your Manager Target stock list, weights, rebalance history, next rebalance date
Subscription Tier You Payment record, subscription status, expiry date
Execution Tier You (per broker) Actual executed trades, quantities, average prices, net portfolio

⚖️Rebalancing Your Model Portfolio

Rebalancing buys and sells stocks to bring your actual holdings in line with your manager's updated target allocation. The app calculates the exact trades needed and guides you through execution.

📅

Rebalances are triggered by your manager. When a new rebalance is published, you'll see an action card on your Home screen and receive a notification.

Step-by-Step Rebalance Flow

1
Rebalance Pending Alert
Home screen shows a yellow/orange rebalance card with strategy name and date. Tap Accept Rebalance.
2
View Current Holdings
App fetches current holdings from your broker. You see existing stocks, quantities, and current values vs the new target weights.
3
Calculate & Review Trade List
Calculation engine computes exact buy/sell quantities based on portfolio value, available cash, and target weights. Review every trade before proceeding.
4
Pre-flight Checks
App automatically verifies: broker session, sufficient funds, sell authorization, exchange validation, surveillance flags (Angel One only).
5
Place Orders
Tap Place Orders. All trades sent to broker simultaneously. Takes 5-30 seconds depending on basket size.
6
Results Screen
Per-stock result shown: Complete, Pending, Rejected. Overall rebalance status updates. If partial, Repair Trades flow becomes available.
Click each step to see the corresponding screen

Understanding Rebalance Results

StatusMeaningNext Action
Accepted All orders placed Nothing — portfolio updated
Partial Some orders rejected Use Repair Trades
Pending AMO scenario; waiting for market Pull to refresh at market open
Failed No orders placed Resolve root cause, retry

Common Rejection Reasons

💰
Insufficient Funds
Add cash to your broker account and retry.
⚠️
Cautionary Listing
Stock is under GSM/ASM surveillance — place manually in broker app.
🔐
Session Expired
Reconnect broker, retry rebalance.
🌙
Service Window
Nightly maintenance (Upstox 12-5:30 AM IST). Auto-detected; soft toast.

🔧Repair Trades

If a rebalance resulted in partial execution — some orders went through but others were rejected — the Repair Trades flow lets you re-attempt only the failed orders without re-running the full rebalance calculation.

1
Identify Partial Rebalance
Rebalance card on Home/Plans shows Retry Rebalance when partial execution is detected. Tap it to begin repair.
2
Pre-populated Failed Trades
Review modal opens pre-filled with only the failed orders (unfilled portion of each stock). Successfully placed orders are excluded automatically. Each row shows a failure-reason chip.
3
Mark as Placed (Optional)
For stocks you've placed manually in your broker app (e.g. cautionary listings), tap the chip and Mark as Placed. Enter actual qty and price. The system records your manual placement.
4
Re-execute Remaining
For stocks to be retried by the app, tap Place Orders. Only the remaining unplaced stocks go through.

📋Orders & Order Book

The Orders tab (bottom nav, 4th icon) shows every order you've placed — pending, completed, and rejected — across all your connected brokers. This is where you check status, cancel pending orders, and review past trades.

Understanding the Order Book

1
Open the Orders Tab
Tap the 📋 icon in the bottom navigation. The order book opens with three tabs: Open, Today, History.
2
Filter by Broker / Status
If you have multiple brokers connected, use the dropdown to show only one broker's orders. Filter by status (Complete / Pending / Rejected / AMO).
3
Read an Order Row
Each row shows: stock symbol, BUY/SELL pill, quantity, price (limit/market), broker, time, and status pill. Tap a row for the full detail screen.
4
Cancel a Pending Order
Open a pending order → tap Cancel Order. The cancellation is sent to your broker. Note: orders in pending state at the exchange (not yet matched) can be cancelled; partially-filled orders cancel only the unfilled portion.
5
Check History & Export
The History tab shows orders from the last 90 days. Tap Export to download a CSV/PDF for record-keeping or tax filing.
Click each step to see the screen

Order Status Reference

StatusMeaningWhat You Can Do
Open / Pending Sent to exchange, waiting to match (limit orders, AMOs before market open) Cancel the order, or wait for execution
Complete Fully matched and executed at the exchange View fill price, total value, broker fees
Partial Only some quantity got filled before the order expired (intra-day liquidity issue) Place a fresh order for the remaining qty, or wait for repair flow in MP
Rejected Exchange or broker rejected the order before any fill Read the rejection reason. Fix the cause and re-place.
AMO After-Market Order, queued for next market open Cancel if needed before market opens at 9:15 AM
Cancelled You or the broker cancelled before execution Place a fresh order if still needed

Reading the Order Detail Screen

Tap any order to see the full detail:

  • Order ID — broker's unique reference (useful when calling broker support)
  • Order Time — when you placed it, plus execution time if filled
  • Avg Price — actual fill price (may differ from market price you saw)
  • Filled / Pending qty — for partial fills, breakdown of completed vs. remaining
  • Source — Recommendation / Model Portfolio / Manual (which flow placed it)
  • Strategy — for MP orders, which model portfolio it belongs to
  • Reason — for rejected orders, the broker's exact error message
⏱️

Cancel-order timing matters. If the exchange has already matched your order (status flips from Pending → Complete in milliseconds), you can no longer cancel it. Once executed, you'd need to place an opposite trade to reverse — which has its own costs and tax implications.

🛡️Sell Authorization (DDPI / TPIN / EDIS)

To sell stocks from your demat account, SEBI requires a one-time (or per-session) authorization. The method varies by broker.

Why This Is Required

Under SEBI's DDPI (Demat Debit and Pledge Instruction) framework, your broker must verify your consent before debiting stocks from your demat account. AlphaQuark handles this as a pre-flight check before any SELL order.

Broker-Specific Methods

BrokerMethodHow It Works
Zerodha DDPI or EDIS DDPI-enabled accounts auto-authorize. Others use one-time EDIS OTP flow.
Angel One DDPI or TPIN DDPI-enabled skip the flow. Others enter TPIN in the Angel One TPIN modal.
Dhan TPIN Pre-flight TPIN check before each sell session.
Fyers TPIN TPIN modal shown when SELL orders are rejected without prior auth.
Others (8) Portal-specific Generic auth modal opens if SELL orders are rejected. May require setup in broker's own app first.
📋

DDPI Setup (Recommended for Zerodha & Angel One): Enabling DDPI eliminates per-session authorization. One-time setup in your broker's portal. Once enabled, AlphaQuark detects it and skips the TPIN modal for all future SELL orders.

If you see trades you didn't authorize, an unfamiliar broker connected, or a subscription you didn't make:

  • Immediately email admin@alphaquark.in with screenshots
  • Change your AlphaQuark password
  • Contact your broker to review their session logs
  • If money was moved at the broker, file an SEBI SCORES complaint

Data Retention

Your account data is retained as long as your account is active. On account closure, personally identifiable data is deleted within 90 days. Audit logs (required by SEBI) are retained for 7 years.

❓Frequently Asked Questions

My broker shows "Expired" — what do I do?

Tap Reconnect on the broker card on the Home screen. For OAuth brokers, you'll be taken through a quick browser login. For credential brokers, you'll be prompted for TOTP/PIN. The app uses Smart Re-auth for most brokers so you won't need to re-enter your API key.

Why was my rebalance order rejected?

The most common reasons are: (1) Insufficient funds — add cash to your broker account. (2) Sell authorization not set up — complete TPIN/DDPI/EDIS. (3) Session expired — reconnect broker. (4) Cautionary listing — stock under surveillance, place manually in broker app.

Can I be connected to multiple brokers at the same time?

Yes. AlphaQuark supports multiple simultaneous broker connections. Each broker's portfolio, funds, and orders are tracked independently. You can switch between brokers when executing trades.

What happens if I force-quit the app during payment?

The app has a payment recovery mechanism. On your next launch, it detects the incomplete payment, verifies with the payment gateway, and completes your subscription activation automatically — without charging you again.

I'm getting "Broker service window" errors at night — is this normal?

Yes. Some brokers (particularly Upstox) perform nightly maintenance between 12 AM and 5:30 AM IST during which funds and order placement APIs are unavailable. The app detects these known windows and shows a friendly "try after market open" message rather than an error.

How do I know my credentials are safe?

Your broker API keys and secrets are encrypted with AES-256-CBC before storage. Your broker password is never seen or stored by AlphaQuark — for OAuth brokers, authentication happens directly on the broker's own servers.

What is a Repair Trade?

If a rebalance resulted in partial execution (some orders went through, some were rejected), the Repair Trade flow lets you re-attempt only the failed orders. The calculation engine figures out the exact remaining quantities. You don't need to re-run the full rebalance.

I unsubscribed from a strategy — can I see my past trades?

Yes. Unsubscribing removes you from future rebalance signals but does not delete your executed trade history. Your past trades, quantities, and P&L remain visible in the Portfolio screen.

What does AMO mean?

AMO stands for After Market Order. If you place a trade outside market hours (9:15 AM – 3:30 PM IST), the order is tagged AMO and queued for execution when the market next opens. AMO orders show an amber AMO badge in your order book.
